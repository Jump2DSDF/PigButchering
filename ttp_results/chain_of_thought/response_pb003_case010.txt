**Reasoning:**
The scammer established themselves as a "WhatsApp friend" who provided "instructions" on trading - this suggests they presented themselves as an experienced, successful trader or investment mentor.

Key psychological tactics evident:

2. **Authority/Mentorship**: The scammer gave "instructions" positioning themselves as knowledgeable and authoritative about crypto trading.

3. **Social Proof**: By claiming to know about a profitable trading platform, the scammer implied they had experience or insider knowledge.

5. **Sunk Cost Exploitation**: After victim deposited funds, the tax demand leveraged the psychological pressure to "pay more to get back what you already invested."

The wealth indicators are implied: the scammer positioned themselves as someone who successfully trades crypto for profit, suggesting they have the financial knowledge and success to guide others.

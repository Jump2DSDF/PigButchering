--------------------------------------------------
This case demonstrates an extremely predatory withdrawal barrier pattern:

Barrier 1 - Loan Repayment Demand:

  - Illegal threat of lawsuit for fabricated loan

Forced Retirement Account Liquidation:
  - Victim withdrew money from 401(k) retirement account
  - Purpose: To cover the $50,000 "loan"
  - Result: Victim suffered heavy withdrawal penalties from 401(k)
  - Promise: Trading gains would be sufficient to cover penalties
  - This represents destruction of victim's retirement security

Barrier 2 - Tax Fee Demand:
  - After repaying "loan" from 401(k), victim tried to withdraw profits again
  - New barrier: Must pay 10% fee to cover taxes
  - Fee must be paid using OUTSIDE FUNDS (cannot deduct from account)
  - 10% of profits would be substantial given claimed account value

Communication Cutoff:

  - Complete ghosting at critical moment
  - Victim unable to withdraw any funds
  - Left with $50,000 loan "repaid" from retirement account + penalties

Total Impact on This Victim:

  - Lost future retirement growth on that $50,000

  - Potential tax consequences on 401(k) withdrawal

  - Estimated total loss: $60,000+ including penalties and lost retirement value

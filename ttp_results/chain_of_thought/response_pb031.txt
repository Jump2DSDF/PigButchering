CASE INFORMATION:
- Case ID: 84 (pb_case_id: 31)
- Primary Subject: Horizon Pro Financial
- Website: horizonprofinancial.com
- Scam Type: Pig Butchering Scam / Fraudulent Trading Platform

COMPLAINT NARRATIVE:
"A California resident reports meeting an individual through the app TikTok, who convinced the resident to invest in cryptocurrency on a crypto asset exchange operating on the website horizonprofinancial.com. After depositing funds and trading, the resident attempt to withdraw, but the platform (Horizon Pro Financial) denied the request, stating that a fee was required. To date, the resident has not recovered any of their invested funds. The website is no longer operational."

================================================================================

STEP 1: INITIAL CONTACT ANALYSIS (접근 및 유인)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Reasoning:
The narrative explicitly states that the victim "met an individual through the app TikTok." This is the initial contact platform. TikTok as an initial contact vector is somewhat unusual in pig butchering scams, which more commonly use dating apps, WhatsApp, or professional networking platforms. However, TikTok has increasingly been exploited for scams due to its messaging features and large user base.

The scammer "convinced the resident to invest in cryptocurrency," indicating the lure type was an investment opportunity rather than romance or friendship. The narrative does not mention any migration to another communication platform (e.g., WhatsApp, Telegram), though this is common in pig butchering operations. The absence of this information suggests either:
1. Communication stayed on TikTok
2. The victim did not report this detail
3. The migration occurred but wasn't documented

Key Findings:
- Initial Contact Platform: TikTok
- Lure Type: Investment opportunity (cryptocurrency investment)
- Communication Migration: Not specified in narrative (unknown)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

- They were persuasive enough to convince the victim to invest

The relationship type appears to be investment advisor or mentor rather than romantic partner, given the focus on investment rather than personal relationship building. The narrative lacks details about:
- Any claimed identity (name, profession, background)
- Wealth indicators shown (luxury lifestyle, trading profits, etc.)
- Duration of trust-building phase

Psychological tactics evident from the narrative:
1. Trust building - The scammer spent time to "convince" the victim, suggesting rapport development
2. Authority - Presenting knowledge of cryptocurrency and access to a trading platform
3. Fear of Missing Out (FOMO) - Investment opportunities typically exploit this
4. Sunk cost exploitation - After initial deposit, the victim attempted to withdraw, but fees were demanded, a classic tactic to extract more money

Key Findings:
- Claimed Identity: Unknown (not specified)

- Relationship Type: Investment advisor/mentor

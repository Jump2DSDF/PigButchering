### Step 1: Initial Contact Analysis (접근 및 유인)

**Reasoning:**
The narrative states the victim "met an individual online named 'Aileen' and beginning a relationship." This indicates:
- Initial contact was made on an unspecified online platform (likely dating app or social media)
- The relationship was established as romantic or close personal relationship
- The scammer used a specific persona name ("Aileen")
- No explicit mention of platform migration, but the relationship development suggests ongoing communication

The lure type is clearly romance-based that evolved into an investment opportunity. This is textbook Pig Butchering - establishing an intimate relationship before introducing crypto investment.

**Extracted:**
- Initial contact platform: Online (unspecified, likely dating or social media)
- Lure type: Romance → Investment opportunity
- Communication migration: Not explicitly stated (inferred continued online communication)
- Scammer name: "Aileen"

### Step 2: Persona & Psychology Analysis (사칭 및 심리)

**Reasoning:**
Aileen presented a sophisticated persona with specific wealth indicators:
1. **Family wealth story**: "Aunt was a successful investor that owned properties in France and Croatia"
2. **Geographic specificity**: Naming specific European countries adds credibility
3. **Personal success claim**: Claimed she had personally "made money" on the platform

Key psychological tactics identified:

1. **Trust Building**: Established romantic relationship first ("beginning a relationship")
2. **Social Proof**: The aunt's success story provides third-party validation
3. **Authority**: Aunt portrayed as wealthy, successful investor
4. **Reciprocity**: Suggested the victim start investing, implying she was sharing valuable knowledge
5. **Proof of Concept**: Small initial investments with successful withdrawals ("victim was able to withdraw some of their funds")
6. **Greed/FOMO**: Success of small withdrawals encouraged larger deposits
7. **Sunk Cost Exploitation**: After significant investment, demanded $500,000 "security fee"
8. **Progressive Investment**: Started with "small amounts" in "short term savings plan," gradually escalating

The "blockchain savings plan" terminology is designed to sound legitimate and low-risk (like a traditional savings account but with crypto benefits).

**Extracted:**
- Claimed identity: Romantic partner with wealthy, successful investor aunt
- Relationship type: Romantic partner
- Wealth indicators: Properties in France and Croatia, successful investing track record, personal trading success
- Psychological tactics: Trust building, social proof, authority, reciprocity, proof of concept, greed exploitation, sunk cost exploitation
- Trust building period: Extended period (relationship establishment phase)

### Step 3: Fraud Mechanism Analysis (사기 수法)

**Reasoning:**
The platform "web3app.rest" is described as a "crypto asset trading platform" offering a "blockchain savings plan" with "short term savings plan" options.

Critical fraud mechanics identified:

1. **Product naming**: "Blockchain savings plan" - designed to sound safe and traditional
2. **Tiered products**: "Short term savings plan" suggests multiple product offerings
3. **Initial success**: Victim could withdraw from small investments - classic confidence building
4. **Fake profits shown**: Yes - the ability to withdraw small amounts proves the platform displayed accessible funds
5. **Withdrawal blocking**: Final tactic was demanding $500,000 for "security fees"
6. **Platform shutdown**: "Website is no longer operational" - typical exit scam behavior

The withdrawal blocking escalation:
- Phase 1: Small deposits, successful withdrawals (building confidence)
- Phase 2: Larger deposits encouraged by early success
- Phase 3: Massive $500,000 "security fee" demand when victim tried to withdraw
- Phase 4: Complete fund freeze when victim refused
- Phase 5: Platform disappearance

This is a sophisticated operation - the initial withdrawal capability is a critical trust-building mechanism that sets this apart from simpler scams.

**Extracted:**
- Platform type: Fake trading platform (disguised as "blockchain savings plan")
- Platform name: web3app.rest
- Platform URL: web3app.rest
- Fake profits shown: Yes (evidenced by initial successful withdrawals)
- Withdrawal tactics: $500,000 "security fee" demand
- Platform status: Defunct ("no longer operational")
- Product offerings: "Blockchain savings plan," "short term savings plan"

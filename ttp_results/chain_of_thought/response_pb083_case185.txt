STEP 5: MANIPULATION & WITHDRAWAL BARRIERS
-------------------------------------------
The Withdrawal Obstruction:
When the victim attempted to withdraw a larger amount, they encountered the classic pig butchering withdrawal barrier:

Fee Demand Requirements:
1. Pay $1,000 fee to enable withdrawal
2. Send screenshots to ICE customer service (iceservicev@gmail.com)
3. Provide evidence of payment compliance
4. Only then would withdrawal be processed

This represents the standard "fee extraction" phase where scammers demand:
- Withdrawal fees
- Tax payments
- Account verification fees
- Anti-money laundering compliance
- Platform upgrade fees
- Trading insurance

Victim Response:
The victim demonstrated awareness by:
1. Refusing to pay the $1,000 fee
2. Recognizing the illegitimacy of the demand
3. Declining further engagement
4. Accepting inability to recover funds
5. Reporting to authorities

Screenshot Verification Tactic:
Requiring victims to send screenshots serves multiple purposes:
1. Creates appearance of legitimate compliance process
2. Delays withdrawal further
3. Gives scammers time to extract more victims
4. Makes victim jump through hoops, increasing sunk cost fallacy
5. Provides scammers with evidence of victim compliance for future manipulation

Communication Pattern During Withdrawal Phase:
1. Victim requests larger withdrawal
2. Platform/app blocks withdrawal
3. Platform demands $1,000 payment
4. Directs victim to email customer service
5. Requires screenshot evidence of payment
6. Victim refuses and loses access to funds

Red Flags Identified:
1. Successful small withdrawal but large withdrawal blocked
2. Payment required to access own money
3. Arbitrary $1,000 fee with no prior disclosure
4. Screenshot requirement for "verification"
5. Customer service only via Gmail account (not professional domain)
6. No alternative withdrawal method offered

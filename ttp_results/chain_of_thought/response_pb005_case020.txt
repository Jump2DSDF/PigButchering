### Step 3: Fraud Mechanism Analysis (사기 수법)

**Reasoning:**
The fraud mechanism is clearly described and follows classic Pig Butchering patterns:

1. **Platform Type**: "crypto asset trading platform YCBIT" - a fake trading platform designed to simulate real trading activity

2. **Platform Details**:
   - Platform name: YCBIT
   - Platform URL: ycbit.com
   - Current status: "no longer active" (defunct)

3. **Fake Profits**: The narrative states the victim "believed their account had grown in value" - this confirms the platform displayed fake profits to encourage continued investment and create the impression of successful trading.

4. **Withdrawal Blocking Tactics**: This case shows an escalating three-tier demand structure:
   - **Tier 1**: 10% of withdrawal amount as deposit
   - **Tier 2**: 15% of total account balance to "confirm identity"
   - **Tier 3**: 100% of total account balance (equal to the total amount in the account)

This escalating demand pattern is particularly predatory:
- Each demand is framed as necessary to proceed
- The percentages increase, testing the victim's commitment
- The final 100% demand is essentially doubling the victim's perceived holdings
- Each payment is presented as the "last" barrier to accessing funds

The three-tier structure with shifting justifications (withdrawal fee → identity verification → matching deposit) is designed to extract maximum funds while keeping the victim hopeful that access is just one more payment away.

**Extracted:**
- Platform type: Fake crypto trading platform
- Platform names: YCBIT
- Platform URLs: ycbit.com
- Fake profit shown: Yes (confirmed)
- Withdrawal block tactics:
  - 10% withdrawal deposit fee
  - 15% identity confirmation deposit
  - 100% account balance matching deposit
- Platform status: Defunct ("no longer active")

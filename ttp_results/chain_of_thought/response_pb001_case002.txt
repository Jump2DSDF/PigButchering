## Chain of Thought Analysis for Case 002 (b2c2-amm.com)

### Step 1: Initial Contact Analysis (접근 및 유인)

**Reasoning:**
The narrative states the victim was "contacted by an individual online who asked to move the conversation to a messaging platform." This indicates:
- Initial contact was made on an unspecified online platform
- There was deliberate migration to a messaging platform (likely WhatsApp, Telegram, or similar)
- The scammer initiated contact, not the victim

The lure type appears to be friendship-based ("friend") that evolved into an investment opportunity ("joint-investing"). This is classic Pig Butchering - building trust through friendship before introducing the investment angle.

**Extracted:**
- Initial contact platform: Online (unspecified)
- Communication migration: Messaging platform
- Lure type: Friendship → Investment opportunity

---

### Step 2: Persona & Psychology Analysis (사칭 및 심리)

**Reasoning:**
The scammer presented themselves as a "friend" who was also an investor. Key psychological tactics evident:

1. **Trust Building**: Formed a "relationship" before introducing investment
2. **Reciprocity**: Offered "joint-investing" where they would "match" the victim's investment - this creates a sense of partnership and shared risk
3. **Social Proof**: By claiming to invest alongside the victim, the scammer validated the investment
4. **Fear/Urgency**: Used fabricated "data breach" to create urgency for payment
5. **Sunk Cost Exploitation**: After victim paid $77,000, demanded additional $100,000

The "matching investment" claim is a sophisticated trust mechanism - it implies the scammer has skin in the game.

**Extracted:**
- Claimed identity: Friend/Investment partner
- Relationship type: Friend
- Psychological tactics: Trust building, Reciprocity, Fear (data breach), Sunk cost exploitation

---

### Step 3: Fraud Mechanism Analysis (사기 수법)

**Reasoning:**
The platform is described as a "crypto asset trading platform" - this is the typical Pig Butchering infrastructure.

Withdrawal blocking tactics identified:
1. First block: Fabricated "data breach" requiring $77,000 payment
2. Second block: Demanded additional $100,000 "investment"

This dual-barrier approach is designed to:
- Extract maximum funds from the victim
- Make the victim feel they're "close" to getting their money back
- The first payment creates sunk cost psychology for the second demand

Platform status: "no longer operational" - typical for these scams once exposed or after extracting funds.

**Extracted:**
- Platform type: Fake trading platform
- Platform names: b2c2-amm.com
- Withdrawal tactics: Data breach fee ($77,000), Additional investment demand ($100,000)
- Platform status: Defunct

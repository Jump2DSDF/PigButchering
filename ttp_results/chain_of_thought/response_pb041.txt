### Step 2: Persona & Psychology Analysis (Impersonation and Psychology)

**Reasoning:**

**Moses (Victim 1):**
- Claimed identity: Crypto trading teacher/mentor
- Promised to "teach them to make money" - positioned as educator and guide
- Provided step-by-step instructions: account opening, crypto purchase, transfer process
- Demonstrated knowledge and authority in crypto trading

**Vivian (Victim 2):**
- Claimed identity: FRTC Limited Broker representative or affiliate
- More sophisticated operation - helped with account opening AND fund transfers
- Positioned as helpful broker/advisor
- Later offered to "contribute $350,000 toward the taxes owed" - creating false sense of partnership and reciprocity
- Encouraged victim to "increase their capital to generate higher returns" - classic upselling tactic

**Psychological Tactics Identified:**

1. **Trust Building**: Both scammers built relationships on legitimate platforms (WhatsApp, LinkedIn) before introducing the platform
2. **Authority**: Moses as "teacher," Vivian as "broker" - both positioned as experts
3. **Mentorship/Guidance**: Step-by-step instructions created dependency
4. **Social Proof**: Vivian's association with "FRTC Limited Broker" created legitimacy
5. **Fear of Missing Out (FOMO)**: Promises of profits and "higher returns"
6. **Sunk Cost Exploitation**: Victim 2 was shown $1.66M in fake profits, making them invested in continuing
7. **Reciprocity**: Vivian's offer to contribute $350K creates psychological obligation
8. **Urgency**: Sudden loss of $75K (Victim 1) and tax demands (Victim 2) create pressure
9. **Gaslighting**: Platform's claim of "losing everything in just a few minutes" is likely fabricated to justify theft

**Wealth Indicators:**
- Vivian offered to contribute $350K - suggesting personal wealth
- Both scammers positioned as successful traders/brokers
- Platform showed $1.66M in fake profits to Victim 2

**Trust Building Period:**
- Victim 1: Sufficient time for Moses to establish mentor relationship and guide through entire process
- Victim 2: Extended period - victim made "several deposits" over time and was shown accumulating profits, even successfully withdrew $55K once (classic trust-building withdrawal)

The successful $55K withdrawal by Victim 2 is particularly significant - this is a sophisticated tactic where scammers allow a small withdrawal to:
- Build trust and credibility
- Encourage larger deposits
- Create social proof that the platform "works"

**Extracted:**
- Claimed identities: Moses (crypto trading teacher/mentor), Vivian (FRTC broker representative)
- Relationship types: Mentor (Moses), Investment advisor/broker (Vivian)
- Psychological tactics: Trust building, authority, fear_of_missing_out, sunk_cost_exploitation, reciprocity, urgency, gaslighting
- Trust building period: Extended (Victim 2 made multiple deposits over time, allowed one withdrawal of $55K)
- Wealth indicators: Successful trader claims, Vivian's $350K offer, $1.66M in fake profits shown

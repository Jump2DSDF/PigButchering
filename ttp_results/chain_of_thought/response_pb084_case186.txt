STEP 1: CASE UNDERSTANDING & INITIAL CONTACT
---------------------------------------------
Scam Type Classification:
- Fraudulent Trading Platform
- Pig Butchering Scam
- Investment Group Scam

Initial Contact Method:
The victim joined a WhatsApp group chat focused on crypto contract trading, then met "Mona" within this group. This represents a sophisticated multi-vector approach:

1. WhatsApp Group Vector:
   - Investment/trading group chat
   - Crypto contract trading focus
   - Multiple participants (creates social proof)
   - Group dynamic reduces individual skepticism
   - Appears to be peer-to-peer learning environment

2. Individual Contact (Mona):
   - Personal outreach from group member
   - One-on-one coaching relationship
   - Direct guidance and instruction
   - Personalized investment advice

This dual-vector approach (group + individual) is particularly effective because:
- Group provides legitimacy and social validation
- Individual contact provides personalized attention
- Victim sees "others" in group (may be scammer accomplices)
- Creates impression of exclusive investment opportunity
- Combines community trust with expert guidance

Victim Profile:
- California resident
- WhatsApp user
- Interested in cryptocurrency trading
- Responsive to group investment opportunities
- Had disposable funds for crypto investment
- Trusting of group members initially

STEP 5: MANIPULATION & WITHDRAWAL BARRIERS
-------------------------------------------
Withdrawal Attempt:
Once suspicious, the victim attempted to withdraw their money from absgbl.com.

The False Confirmation:
The victim "received a message from the website that the transaction was completed" - this represents a sophisticated deception:

1. System-Generated Confirmation: Platform sent withdrawal success message
2. False Reassurance: Victim believed funds were being processed
3. Technical Credibility: Automated messages suggest legitimate system
4. Psychological Relief: Victim thought they successfully exited
5. Delayed Discovery: Time passed before victim realized funds never arrived

Withdrawal Failure Reality:
Despite the platform confirmation message:
- Victim never received the money
- Funds remained in scammer control
- Confirmation message was fake/meaningless
- No actual blockchain transaction occurred
- Complete loss of deposited funds

This "false confirmation" tactic serves multiple purposes:
- Delays victim's realization of theft
- Prevents immediate escalation or complaints
- Buys time for scammers to exit and cover tracks
- Creates confusion about when theft actually occurred
- Makes victim believe technical issue rather than fraud

Platform Shutdown:
The website absgbl.com is "no longer operational" - classic exit scam pattern:
- Platform collected sufficient funds from multiple victims
- Operators shut down website and disappeared
- All victim funds stolen
- No recourse for recovery
- Group and Mona likely disappeared simultaneously

Red Flags During Withdrawal Phase:
1. Mona's repeated deposit pressure (behavioral change)
2. Withdrawal confirmation message without actual fund receipt
3. Inability to contact platform or Mona after withdrawal attempt
4. Platform eventually becoming non-operational
5. Complete communication blackout
